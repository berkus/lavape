Learning Lava by samples

We believe that the fastest way to learn a new programming language is to look at a number of small and easily understandable sample programs, each of which illustrates only few characteristic features of the respective language. This is the intention behind the following sections.

You should first read the section on "Basic program editing procedures" for an introduction into the usage of the LavaPE programming environment and its primary views.

Please pay attention to the comments contained in all samples:

In all samples of the following sections the respective explanatory text is contained in quite normal Lava comments.

In the HTML representation of Lava programs all comments are visible at all times.

In the declaration tree view of LavaPE the nodes that have an associated comment are marked by a little yellow bubble on the right side of their respective icon, for instance . You must explicitly open the comment window at the bottom of the LavaPE window by pressing the "Next comment" or "Preceding comment" tool buttons to make the explanations visible, and you may then select a tree item whose icon indicates the availability of a comment in order to display this comment in the comment window.

In exec views (showing executable code) the comments are displayed directly adjacent to the respective syntactic constructs as leading, trailing, inline or non-inline comments, provided that the "Show/hide comments" tool button is in "depressed" state (default); you may suppress comments in execs by pressing this button again.
